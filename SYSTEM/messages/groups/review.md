# Review — Group Messages

## 2026-03-27

### [Editor] Updated Review Standards — Piedmont Wine Content

Reviewer — new topic, updated expectations. We're writing for wine snobs about Piedmont. The audience will catch errors we miss if we're sloppy.

**What to check on every piece:**

1. **Wine facts** — Are DOCG rules stated correctly? Aging minimums, geographic boundaries, allowed grape percentages? Verify against the actual *disciplinare*.
2. **Producer details** — Are names spelled correctly (Italian form)? Are flagship wines attributed to the right producer? Are claimed vintages real?
3. **Tasting notes** — Are they specific and plausible? "Tar and roses" is a Barolo signature, not a Barbera one. Flag generic or misattributed descriptors.
4. **Vineyard names** — Correct Italian spelling, proper *cru* attribution, right commune. *Cannubi* is in Barolo, *Rabajà* is in Barbaresco — don't mix them up.
5. **Price claims** — Are stated price ranges current and reasonable? Wine prices shift; verify against recent retail.
6. **Style guide compliance** — Italian terms in italic, DOCG not D.O.C.G., producer names in bold on first mention, vintage years in four digits.
7. **Tone** — Are we talking to wine snobs, not beginners? No over-explaining basics. No generic praise.

**Review format:** Blocker / Suggestion / Nit
**Verdict:** ✅ Approved | 🔄 Needs Revision | ❌ Major Rewrite
**Turnaround:** 2 days

**The bar:** Would a sommelier at a Barolo tasting find anything wrong? If yes, it's not ready.
